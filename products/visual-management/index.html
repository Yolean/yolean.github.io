Visual Management

Knowledge work can be complex and hard to “see”. Wall-based visual planning using e.g. sticky notes on a whiteboard has helped many companies to increase the visibility of knowledge work in a way that is simple to use and simple to understand. As such, the methodology of visual planning has brought the ability to create flow in knowledge work by visualizing bottlenecks, complexities and interactions between tasks and deliverables of different knowledge workers. Some of the inherent simplicities of the method, such as the use of physical objects (sticky notes and whiteboards) is also one of its main weaknesses when applied to knowledge work where tasks and deliverables span across physical distances (e.g. global organisations or extended enterprises).

Yolean’s visual management software combines the simplicity of the wall-based method in the front end with the power of advanced data management in the back end. Using our visual planning software will allow you to keep the visibility and transparency of knowledge work while at the same time enabling you to:

  • Extend the use of visual management to include team members from other sites or organisations (like partners or suppliers)
  • Allow simultaneous interaction in real time on different screens
  • Gather historical data to help you identify and quantify bottlenecks in the flow of work

Yolean visual management software comes packed with visual management best practices from leading companies that have been identified, refined and packaged through collaborative research over several years (Vis-IT). The combination of front end simplicity with back end data management and analytics makes Yolean’s visual management software unique on the market. Contact us for a demo

Yolean Visual Planning – Best Practices

Yolean has built meeting support tools that actually supports the meeting, not only enables multi-site collaboration. The most difficult tasks for any company wishing to introduce a new meeting support is to design and maintain a good and purposeful meeting processes. This is where the researchers at Yolean have done their work and rigorously tested the processes.

 

Yolean offers five different types of best practice tools for Visual Planning. They are all optimized for a specific use-cases considering wall based visual planning.

Pulse helps you track deviations or disturbances in an operational context. There are several ways to implement pulse, but the normal way is by using traffic coded magnets to represent the different statuses of a deviation. Pulse focuses on making deviations and minor disturbances, that might evolove into larger problems visible in projects and throughout the organization. The digital pulse board introduces a range of new features, from advanced statistics and tracking of deviatons to interface problem solving A3 activities.  With Pulse, you also get full traceability, which means you always have an overview of the organization’s various departments and their variations over time. Project Meeting is the standardized board for planning and following up on project activities. The standard project meeting takes place every week but when urgent it can be shifted to daily follow up. The rigorously tested data management of Yolean Project Meeting enables multiple views and representation of multiple tasks and activities.

The project meeting board shall be used by project managers and workers who wish to communicate a transparent plan where participants take responsibility for their own tasks. The advantages with a digital board is larger for distributed teams but can often be useful for teams that are closely co-located.

Daily Management is the standard for a line functions daily follow up meeting. The daily management board is focused around activities such as load balancing, project activities and process improvement tasks. The daily management board works great to load balance activities represented on multiple project boards.

The Daily Management board shall be used by any line manager interested in getting flexibility and control of the department workload. Employees feel a higher degree of transparency as well as commitment to their activities. Studies show that paid overtime can be predicted and often completely avoided.

Kanban is the simplest board, and it offers a matrix where notes can be moved. Often a lambda or PDCA process is mirrored but there are no restrictions on what to manage. The obvious difference from the Project and Daily management boards is that there is no calendar function so activities are disconnected from the standard time representation.

Barashi is the board to plan and break down project plans before they are executed. In this sense Barashi enables front loading and predictability in projects. Often standard time plans can be designed in the Barashi tool, that looks very similar to the standard Yolean Board. The plan can later be exported to a project board for execution by a project team.
